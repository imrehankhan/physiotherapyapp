Slip Disc

A slip disc, also known as a herniated or ruptured disc, happens when one of the soft, cushion-like discs between the bones in your spine gets damaged. These discs act like shock absorbers, helping your spine move smoothly. When a disc "slips," it means the inner jelly-like part of the disc has pushed out through a crack in the tougher outer layer. This can put pressure on nearby nerves, leading to discomfort.
